## In-Depth Report

The ongoing national scrutiny over the National Eligibility-cum-Entrance Test (NEET) examination has taken a significant turn, with Delhi Police making serious allegations regarding external interference. Authorities have claimed to have identified over 400 social media handles, purportedly originating from Pakistan, that were actively attempting to fuel and exacerbate the protests against the NEET-UG 2024 results. This revelation brings to light a potential nexus between domestic unrest and alleged cross-border disinformation campaigns.

### The Allegations: Op Sindoor and Pakistan Handles

According to senior officials within the Delhi Police, these identified handles are not merely isolated instances but are part of a larger, coordinated operation. This operation, reportedly code-named "Op Sindoor," is described by authorities as an initiative aimed at leveraging existing societal grievances and public discontent within India to sow discord and promote narratives that could destabilize the nation. The alleged tactics involve circulating misinformation, fabricating content, and strategically amplifying genuine grievances to manipulate public opinion and incite further unrest. The Delhi Police's Intelligence Fusion & Strategic Operations (IFSO) unit is reportedly spearheading the investigation into these digital activities.

The focus of these alleged external actors, as stated by the police, was to capitalize on the widespread student protests and concerns over alleged paper leaks and irregularities in the NEET examination results. By using keywords and trending hashtags related to NEET, these accounts allegedly sought to inject divisive narratives, portray the Indian government and institutions in a negative light, and call for broader civil disobedience beyond the scope of educational reform. The police have indicated that these handles primarily operated across platforms such as X (formerly Twitter), Facebook, and Instagram, using a mix of fake profiles, bot networks, and compromised accounts to maximize their reach.

### Orchestrated Amplification Amidst Genuine Concerns

The timing of this revelation is particularly noteworthy, as it comes amidst widespread student protests and legal challenges demanding a re-examination of NEET-UG 2024 due to allegations of paper leaks and discrepancies in the awarding of grace marks. While acknowledging the legitimacy of some student concerns, the Delhi Police assert that the identified foreign handles sought to co-opt and manipulate these genuine grievances. The objective, as described by authorities, was not to support the students' cause but to exploit the situation for broader geopolitical aims, potentially undermining public trust in national institutions and governance.

The investigation reportedly involves sophisticated digital forensics to trace the origins of these accounts, analyze their posting patterns, and identify the content they disseminated. While specific details about the content and nature of disinformation were not fully disclosed due to ongoing investigations, officials hinted at attempts to create false narratives, share doctored images, and propagate conspiracy theories surrounding the examination process and the government's response. This alleged digital interference presents a significant challenge to maintaining the integrity of public discourse during periods of national concern.

## Background & Context

The NEET-UG 2024 examination, conducted by the National Testing Agency (NTA) on May 5, quickly became a focal point of national controversy following the declaration of results on June 4. The unprecedented number of perfect scores (67 candidates scoring 720/720), coupled with allegations of question paper leaks in certain centres and the contentious awarding of grace marks to over 1,500 candidates, ignited widespread protests across the country. Students, parents, and various political organizations demanded re-examination and a thorough investigation into the alleged irregularities.

These protests escalated, with petitions filed in the Supreme Court and High Courts challenging the results and the NTA's conduct. The Union Education Ministry and the NTA have been under intense pressure, leading to the cancellation of grace marks and a provision for re-examination for affected candidates. The government also formed a high-level committee to review the NTA's functioning and recommend improvements, acknowledging the gravity of the situation and the anguish among the student community.

In the broader context, India has previously reported instances of alleged foreign interference and disinformation campaigns targeting its internal affairs. Such campaigns, often attributed to hostile state actors or their proxies, aim to exploit existing socio-political fault lines, spread propaganda, and influence public opinion. The digital landscape, particularly social media platforms, has become a key battleground for such 'hybrid warfare,' where information and perception management play a critical role. The Delhi Police's current claims, if substantiated, would fit into this evolving pattern of external attempts to meddle in India's domestic issues, leveraging moments of national vulnerability.

## Why It Matters (Impact Analysis)

The Delhi Police's allegations of "400 Pak handles" and "Op Sindoor" in connection with the NEET protests carry significant implications for public discourse, national security, and the future of digital governance in India. Firstly, it adds a complex and potentially contentious layer to an already sensitive issue. While the genuine concerns of students regarding examination integrity remain paramount, the alleged involvement of foreign actors could potentially shift the narrative, raising questions about the motivations behind certain amplified voices and information shared online.

This development also underscores the growing threat of information warfare and the manipulation of social media in contemporary democracies. It highlights how external elements can exploit internal dissent and leverage digital platforms to spread misinformation, polarize opinions, and potentially destabilize social harmony. For the public, it reinforces the need for critical evaluation of information, particularly during periods of heightened emotion and controversy, and raises awareness about the potential for malicious actors to infiltrate and distort online conversations.

Furthermore, this revelation could have implications for India's foreign relations, particularly with Pakistan, if definitive evidence emerges linking state-sponsored entities to these alleged campaigns. Domestically, it might lead to increased scrutiny of social media platforms and calls for more robust mechanisms to identify and counter foreign interference. This could spark debates about balancing national security concerns with freedom of expression, potentially influencing future regulatory frameworks for online content and digital activism in India.
